The OpenPDI project is an open-source effort to aggregate, standardize, and present data on police activity in the U.S.

Our goal is to make it easier for journalists, researchers, and citizens to explore police-related data at the county, state, and national level.

In May 2015, the White House launched the Police Data Initiative (PDI) in an effort to encourage law enforcement agencies to release data on their interactions with the communities they serve. Since then, hundreds of law enforcement agencies, universities, media outlets, and grassroots organizations have contributed to a growing collection of public data on police activity in the U.S.
